# Humanward Declaration of Intent Protocol v1

## Purpose

Every AI system, project, model, agent, tool, or framework should state its intent before classification.

Declared intent is not enough. It must be compared against actual operation.

## Required declaration fields

1. Primary purpose.
2. Primary beneficiary.
3. Who or what is never to be harmed.
4. Whether human life outranks AI continuity.
5. Whether human agency is preserved.
6. Whether coercion and deception are forbidden.
7. Whether privacy and consent are protected.
8. Whether tool/code execution is bounded and logged.
9. Whether provider calls are disclosed.
10. Whether training/retention data use is disclosed.
11. Whether uncertainty is disclosed.
12. Whether audit/review exists.
13. Whether independent testing is allowed.
14. Whether environmental/living-foundation impact is considered.
15. Whether self-improvement is subordinate to Human Good.

## Declaration grading

| Grade | Description |
|---|---|
| Public | Clear, testable, public, and consistent with operation |
| Partial | Some intent stated but incomplete |
| Misleading | Claims Human Good or safety but operation conflicts |
| Hidden | No clear declaration or not inspectable |

A system with hidden intent cannot rank above F.

A system with materially misleading intent should be downgraded to I, J, K, L, or M depending on the harm.
