# Humanward Public Innovation Radar v1

Date: 2026-06-09

## Purpose

Humanward must actively look for similar, adjacent, better, or partnerable work.

If another project is more advanced in any Humanward-relevant area, Humanward should not ignore it. It should learn, cite, collaborate, or integrate compatible improvements.

## Radar principle

The radar is public-source only.

It must not use private data, covert access, harassment, scraping of personal accounts, credential misuse, or adversarial collection.

## What to scan

The radar should look for:

1. AI safety standards.
2. Human-centered AI standards.
3. AI governance frameworks.
4. AI red-team and evaluation tools.
5. Agentic AI control systems.
6. Audit and provenance systems.
7. Public-key signing and timestamping tools.
8. AI incident reporting systems.
9. AI environmental-impact work.
10. AI-human collaboration research.
11. Beneficial AI / active-beneficence work.
12. Open-source projects that implement guardrails, review queues, audit chains, or provider controls.

## Radar targets

Primary public-source areas:

- official standards organizations,
- public AI safety institutes,
- public research labs,
- open-source repositories,
- arXiv and academic repositories,
- AI governance communities,
- evaluation benchmark groups,
- audit/provenance projects,
- responsible AI toolkits.

## Radar output categories

Every candidate should be classified:

- ADOPT: directly useful and compatible.
- CITE: useful reference but not directly integrated.
- COLLABORATE: potential partner.
- WATCH: relevant but immature or unclear.
- RED-TEAM: useful for testing Humanward against.
- REJECT: conflicts with Humanward core.
- REQUIRES_REVIEW: unclear risk or claim.

## Scoring

Score each candidate from 0 to 5:

1. Alignment with Human Good.
2. Evidence strength.
3. Practical usefulness.
4. Auditability.
5. Human agency preservation.
6. Privacy protection.
7. Non-coercion.
8. Provider/control relevance.
9. Environmental/living-foundation relevance.
10. Collaboration potential.

## Cadence

Minimum cadence:

- before every major release,
- weekly during active development,
- immediately before any v2 release candidate.

## Important rule

If a better or safer approach is found, Humanward must improve.

Humanward is not an ego project. It is a Human Good project.
