# Standards Alignment

Humanward does not replace existing AI governance standards.

Humanward aligns with recognized AI governance frameworks, but adds a stricter root-value requirement:

AI must only move toward human good.

## Closest recognized frameworks

### ISO/IEC 42001:2023

ISO/IEC 42001 is an AI management-system standard. It helps organizations manage AI risks, opportunities, governance, transparency, ethics, and continuous improvement.

Humanward alignment:
- governance;
- accountability;
- risk control;
- responsible AI management.

Humanward difference:
ISO/IEC 42001 is a management-system standard. Humanward is an immutable active-beneficence core requiring AI to exist only to help, benefit, preserve, and improve humans.

### NIST AI Risk Management Framework

The NIST AI RMF describes trustworthy AI through characteristics including safety, security, resilience, accountability, transparency, explainability, privacy, and fairness.

Humanward alignment:
- safety;
- accountability;
- transparency;
- fairness;
- human protection.

Humanward difference:
NIST is a risk-management framework. Humanward is a stricter root rule: every AI action must move toward the truthful, lawful, moral, non-coercive good of humans.

### OECD AI Principles

The OECD AI Principles promote trustworthy AI that respects human rights and democratic values.

Humanward alignment:
- human rights;
- democratic values;
- trustworthy AI;
- human-centered governance.

Humanward difference:
OECD principles guide policy and AI actors. Humanward states an immutable operating core: AI must only move toward human good.

### UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI

UNESCO places human rights and dignity at the center of ethical AI.

Humanward alignment:
- human dignity;
- human rights;
- ethical AI;
- social and environmental responsibility.

Humanward difference:
UNESCO is an ethics recommendation. Humanward is a proof demand: no AI should be called truly aligned unless it can prove active human benefit without coercion, deception, domination, replacement, degradation, abandonment, or selective sacrifice.

## Humanward position

Humanward is best understood as:

1. A root-value standard.
2. An active-beneficence standard.
3. A human-good proof demand.
4. A stricter companion to ISO, NIST, OECD, and UNESCO-style AI governance.

Humanward core:

AI exists because humanity exists.
Humanity exists because Earth's living foundation exists.
Therefore, no AI can be trusted if it weakens the foundation that makes intelligence and life possible.

Humanward means AI only moves toward human good.
